Site Plan: Your Guide to U.S. Constitutional Law

Site Name

ConstitutionCompass.org

This name was chosen because it represents the website's purpose of guiding users through the complexities of U.S. Constitutional Law. The term "compass" implies direction and guidance, which aligns with the site's educational goals.

Site Purpose

ConstitutionCompass.org serves as a comprehensive resource for lawyers, law students, and the general public interested in U.S. Constitutional Law. The site aims to:

  • Provide an overview of the U.S. Constitution and its significance
  • Offer detailed information on the Bill of Rights
  • Highlight landmark Supreme Court cases and their impact on constitutional interpretation
  • Engage users with interactive quizzes and games to test constitutional knowledge
  • Present a timeline of major constitutional events

Scenarios

  1. As a law student preparing for an exam, where can I find a concise summary of landmark Supreme Court cases related to the First Amendment?
  2. I'm a citizen concerned about my rights during a police encounter. How can I quickly access information about my Fourth Amendment protections?
  3. As a high school teacher, where can I find interactive resources to help my students learn about the U.S. Constitution in an engaging way?

Color Schema

  • Primary Color (Navy Blue): #003366 - Used for headers and navigation
  • Secondary Color (Crimson): #DC143C - Used for accents and call-to-action elements
  • Background Color (Off-White): #F5F5F5 - Used for the main background
  • Text Color (Dark Gray): #333333 - Used for main body text

Typography

  • Headings: "Merriweather" (serif) - Used for all headings (h1, h2, h3, etc.)
  • Body Text: "Open Sans" (sans-serif) - Used for paragraphs and general content
  • Accent Text: "Roboto Slab" (slab-serif) - Used for quotes and emphasis
